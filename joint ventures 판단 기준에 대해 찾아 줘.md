### 3. Joint Venture 회계처리 방법

> Judgement is exercised to define criteria to identify categories more granularly. 
In applying a consistent policy to each category of investment, we believe IAS 27 would consider the 
categorisation of investments in subsidiaries, separate to associates and joint ventures; therefore, 
categorisation and the measurement of similar categories does not need to be consistent between classes 
of investments. 
How we see it 
While ‘category’ is not defined, we believe that investments in subsidiaries, associates, and joint ventures 
can be further divided into more granular categories, only if a category can be defined clearly and 
objectively and results in information that is relevant and reliable.

### 5. Ch.13 Joint arrangements · PDF p.977 (score=0.0161)

> the scope of IFRS 2. 
How we see it 
When the transaction is outside the scope of IFRS 2, we believe the joint venture measures the non-
monetary asset received at its cost, which is the fair value of the consideration given. In the rare cases 
when the fair value of the asset received by the joint venture does not equal the fair value of the 
consideration given (whether cash or shares), an assessment of all facts and circumstances is needed 
to determine whether it is appropriate to recognise a capital contribution or whether other accounting 
applies. These facts and circumstances include the actions and obligations of the other venturers, 
other services provided by the venturer, future rebates on delivery of goods and relationships between 
the venturers.
